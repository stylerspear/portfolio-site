The Project

This project spawned out of a real problem my coworkers experience on a day to day basis with the web app we use to clock in and out of work. The main issue with the application is that it allows someone who has already clocked in to work to clock in a second time later in the day when they were actually attempting to click the "clock out" button. Unfortunately there is no "undo" feature so the only way to correct the time sheet is to ask a manager to go in and manually adjust the time.


I think this is a simple problem to fix and in doing so it would assist many frustrated users, as well as their managers who won't have to spend their time fixing a mistake caused by poor user design.

The Problem

As previously stated the problem is the ability for an end user to mistakenly clock in or out twice in a row, thereby putting an invalid time entry on their time sheet. The clock in and out buttons are right next to one another and look identical other than their verbiage. Below is a screenshot of the current user interface.



The Solution

My proposed solution is simple, but an effective one that will eliminate the possibility of clicking clock in or out twice in a row. In my design, once a user has clocked in, when they navigate back to the site when clocking out for the day, they will only have the clock out button available. Similarly, when they go to clock back in the next day only the "Clock In" option will be available.
